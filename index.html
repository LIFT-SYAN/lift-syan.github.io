About

The LIFT Lab conducts research in rotorcraft aerodynamics, aircraft icing, advanced aerial mobility, and high-performance computational fluid dynamics. Our work combines experiments, modeling, and computation for challenging aerospace flow problems.

Research

Rotorcraft Aerodynamics

Rotor performance, wake interaction, downwash, and aeroacoustics.

Icing Physics

Engine and airframe icing, thermal protection, and adverse weather safety.

AAM Safety

Advanced Aerial Mobility performance, operations, and certification-relevant studies.

GPU CFD

Lattice Boltzmann methods and accelerated simulation for complex flow environments.

Experimental Aerodynamics

Hardware-triggered synced data acquisition, flow visualization, particle tracking, infrared measurement and high-frequency force and acceleration quantification.

Openings

Postdoctoral Researcher

We are seeking a self-motivated researcher with a strong work ethic and a proven research record. Applicants should have published at least two journal papers in reputable non-predatory Scimago Q1 journals.

Experience in one or more of the following areas is desired: CUDA programming; open-source CFD tools such as OpenFOAM, SU2, or OpenLB; strong experience with commercial CFD packages such as ANSYS or STAR-CCM+; or signal acquisition and processing for wind tunnel experiments with real-time data acquisition systems.

Experience with infrared measurement, flow visualization, and particle tracking is also preferred.

PhD Position

We are seeking an experimental PhD student with a strong background in mechatronics.

Applicants holding a master’s degree are preferred. Outstanding undergraduate applicants with exceptional academic records may also be considered.

Experience with UAV systems, PCB design, and RF circuit design is preferred. Familiarity with NI LabVIEW and Simulink is also preferred.
